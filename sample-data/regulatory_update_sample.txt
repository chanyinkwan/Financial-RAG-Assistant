FINANCIAL CONDUCT AUTHORITY
Policy Statement PS25/3 -- ESG Disclosure Requirements for Wealth Managers
Published: 22 January 2025

1. SUMMARY

The FCA is introducing mandatory ESG disclosure requirements for FCA-authorised
wealth management firms under the Sustainability Disclosure Requirements (SDR)
regime. This policy statement sets out final rules following Consultation Paper
CP23/16 and subsequent industry feedback.

2. SCOPE

These requirements apply to all FCA-authorised firms providing discretionary
or advisory investment management services to retail and professional clients,
with assets under management exceeding GBP 5 billion at firm level.

3. KEY REQUIREMENTS

3.1 Product-Level Disclosures: Firms must publish sustainability entity reports
    for each managed portfolio strategy, including Scope 1, 2, and 3 carbon
    metrics where data is available.

3.2 Client-Facing Communications: All client reports, quarterly reviews, and
    marketing materials must include a standardised ESG summary panel showing
    the portfolio's sustainability rating on the FCA's five-tier classification
    (Tier 1: Not Promoted as Sustainable through to Tier 5: Sustainability Impact).

3.3 Anti-Greenwashing: Firms must not use sustainability-related terms in fund
    or portfolio names unless the strategy meets the corresponding SDR label
    criteria. Existing products have until 30 September 2025 to comply or rename.

4. EFFECTIVE DATES AND COMPLIANCE DEADLINES

  - Naming and marketing rules: 30 September 2025
  - Product-level sustainability disclosures: 31 December 2025
  - Entity-level reporting (annual): First report due 30 June 2026
  - Client-facing ESG summary panels: Must appear on all reports issued
    from 1 January 2026 onwards

5. ENFORCEMENT

Non-compliance may result in supervisory action including restrictions on
new client onboarding, mandatory corrective disclosures, and financial
penalties under the FCA's Decision Procedure and Penalties Manual (DEPP).

Firms are advised to begin gap analysis immediately and engage compliance
teams in mapping current disclosures against the new SDR framework.
